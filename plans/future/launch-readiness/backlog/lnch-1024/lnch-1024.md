# lnch-1024: On-Call Playbook

## Context

Phase 1 (Critical path — must be complete before launch).

The on-call playbook is the single most important operational document. It is the first thing an on-call engineer reads when woken up at 3am by a PagerDuty alert. It must be clear, scannable, and contain direct links to all other runbooks. Without it, incident response relies entirely on individual memory, which degrades severely under stress and context switching. This document is a prerequisite to the platform serving any production traffic.

## Goal

Write a comprehensive on-call playbook at `docs/operations/oncall-playbook.md` that gives any engineer on the rotation the information they need to respond effectively to incidents.

## Non-Goals

- Do not document every possible failure mode in detail here — link to specific runbooks instead.
- Do not define the on-call schedule or rotation (that is a team process, not a doc).
- Do not duplicate content from other runbooks — link to them.

## Scope

**Output file:** `docs/operations/oncall-playbook.md`

**Content outline:**

1. **On-Call Overview** — what it means to be on-call, expectations, escalation path
2. **Emergency contacts** — names, roles, preferred contact method (phone/Slack), escalation order
3. **System architecture quick reference** — one-paragraph description + diagram link of the system
4. **Severity levels**
   | Severity | Description | Response Time | Escalation |
   |----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
   | SEV1 | Complete outage | 15 min | Immediate |
   | SEV2 | Major degradation | 30 min | 1 hour |
   | SEV3 | Minor impact | 4 hours | Next business day |
   | SEV4 | Low priority | Next business day | N/A |
5. **First response checklist** — steps to take in the first 5 minutes of any incident
6. **Incident classification** — link to lnch-1025
7. **Runbook index** — organized table linking every operational runbook by category
8. **Communication channels** — which Slack channels, when to post, template for incident announcement
9. **Declaring and resolving incidents** — how to open and close an incident ticket
10. **Post-incident** — link to post-mortem template (lnch-1026), SLA reporting
11. **Escalation procedures** — link to lnch-1028

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Playbook exists at `docs/operations/oncall-playbook.md`
- [ ] All severity levels and response times are defined (matching PLAN.md table)
- [ ] Emergency contacts are populated (not placeholder names)
- [ ] Runbook index links to all runbooks in this epic
- [ ] First response checklist is actionable within 5 minutes
- [ ] Reviewed and approved by the engineering lead and at least one on-call engineer
- [ ] Walk-through completed with the first on-call rotation before launch
