# lnch-1036: Data Retention Policy

## Context

Phase 2 (Before soft launch).

A data retention policy defines how long different types of data are kept and what happens when the retention period expires. Without this policy, data accumulates indefinitely, increasing storage costs and regulatory risk. GDPR and similar regulations require that user data not be kept longer than necessary. This is a quick win (Size S) and is referenced by lnch-1023 (S3 Lifecycle Management), lnch-1022 (Cognito User Management), and lnch-1037 (Data Privacy).

## Goal

Write a data retention policy at `docs/operations/security/data-retention.md` that defines retention periods for all data types, deletion procedures, and compliance rationale.

## Non-Goals

- Do not implement deletion automation (separate engineering work).
- Do not document full GDPR compliance (that is lnch-1037).
- Do not define data classification beyond what is needed for retention decisions.

## Scope

**Output file:** `docs/operations/security/data-retention.md`

**Content outline:**

1. **Policy purpose** — regulatory basis (GDPR, etc.), business rationale
2. **Data types inventory and retention table**
   | Data Type | Storage Location | Retention Period | Deletion Method | Legal Basis |
   |-----------|-----------------|------------------|-----------------|-------------|
   | User accounts | Cognito + Aurora | Until account deletion + 30 days | Cognito delete + DB purge | Contractual |
   | MOC instruction files | S3 | Until user deletion + 30 days | S3 lifecycle | Contractual |
   | Application logs | CloudWatch | 90 days | CloudWatch auto-expiry | Operational |
   | Audit logs (CloudTrail) | S3 | 1 year | S3 lifecycle | Compliance |
   | Analytics events | TBD | TBD | TBD | Legitimate interest |
3. **User deletion procedure** — what must be deleted when a user requests deletion (GDPR Right to Erasure)
4. **Data minimization** — what data we do NOT collect and why
5. **Backup retention** — Aurora snapshots, S3 versioning retention
6. **Review schedule** — how often this policy is reviewed (annually minimum)
7. **Policy exceptions** — process for requesting a retention exception (legal hold)

## Acceptance Criteria
