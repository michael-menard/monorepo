# lnch-1028: Escalation Procedures

## Context

Phase 3 (Before general availability).

When the on-call engineer cannot resolve an incident within the expected response time, or when the incident exceeds their technical scope, escalation is needed. Without documented escalation paths, engineers hesitate to escalate (worrying about waking people unnecessarily) or do not know who to contact for specific domains (database, infra, security). This document defines exactly who to contact, when, and how.

## Goal

Write an escalation procedures document at `docs/operations/incidents/escalation-procedures.md` defining clear escalation paths, contacts, and criteria for all incident domains.

## Non-Goals

- Do not define the on-call schedule or rotation tool (PagerDuty config).
- Do not duplicate communication templates (lnch-1027).
- Do not define severity levels (lnch-1025).

## Scope

**Output file:** `docs/operations/incidents/escalation-procedures.md`

**Content outline:**

1. **Escalation principles** — when in doubt, escalate; no blame for escalating; faster is better for SEV1
2. **Escalation trigger criteria** — time-based (SEV1: escalate if not resolved in 15 min), severity-based, scope-based (data loss, security)
3. **Escalation contacts by domain**
   - API/Lambda issues — primary and secondary contacts
   - Database/Aurora issues — DBA contact, AWS Support tier
   - Frontend/CDN issues — frontend lead contact
   - Security incidents — security contact, legal if data breach
   - Infrastructure/VPC issues — infra lead contact
   - AWS platform issues — AWS Support escalation procedure
4. **How to escalate** — preferred contact method (phone vs Slack vs PagerDuty) per scenario
5. **What to provide when escalating** — incident summary template, what information to have ready
6. **AWS Support escalation** — how to open a support case, what tier to use for each severity
7. **Executive escalation** — when and how to involve management (SEV1 extended, data breach)
8. **Post-escalation handoff** — transferring incident commander role

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Document exists at `docs/operations/incidents/escalation-procedures.md`
- [ ] Escalation contacts are populated with real names/roles (not placeholders)
- [ ] Time-based escalation criteria are specific (minutes, not "soon")
- [ ] AWS Support escalation procedure is accurate
- [ ] Reviewed and approved by all named escalation contacts
