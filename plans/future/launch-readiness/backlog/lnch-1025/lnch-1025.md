# lnch-1025: Incident Classification Guide

## Context

Phase 1 (Critical path — must be complete before launch).

Consistent incident classification ensures the right people are paged, the right response time is applied, and SLA reporting is accurate. Without a clear guide, engineers under pressure will either over-classify (causing alert fatigue) or under-classify (delaying critical responses). This is a quick win (Size S) that has outsized impact on the team's operational effectiveness. It is referenced by the on-call playbook (lnch-1024).

## Goal

Write a concise incident classification guide at `docs/operations/incidents/classification.md` that gives clear, scenario-based criteria for assigning SEV1 through SEV4.

## Non-Goals

- Do not document post-incident procedures (lnch-1026).
- Do not document escalation contacts (lnch-1028).
- Do not define the on-call schedule.

## Scope

**Output file:** `docs/operations/incidents/classification.md`

**Content outline:**

1. **Overview** — why classification matters, who uses this guide, when to apply it
2. **Severity definitions**
   - SEV1: Complete outage, all users affected, data loss risk — 15 min response
   - SEV2: Major degradation, significant user subset affected or key feature unavailable — 30 min response
   - SEV3: Minor impact, workaround available, limited users affected — 4 hour response
   - SEV4: Low priority, cosmetic/non-functional impact — next business day
3. **Classification decision tree** — flowchart or numbered decision questions
4. **Scenario examples** — at least 3 examples per severity level with correct classification rationale
5. **Gray areas and escalation** — when unsure, default to higher severity, examples of common judgment calls
6. **Reclassification** — how and when to upgrade/downgrade severity during an incident
7. **Impact assessment questions** — "How many users are affected?", "Is data at risk?", "Is there a workaround?"
8. **Quick reference card** — one-page summary suitable for bookmarking

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Guide exists at `docs/operations/incidents/classification.md`
- [ ] All 4 severity levels are defined with response times (matching PLAN.md)
- [ ] At least 3 scenario examples per severity level
- [ ] Decision tree or decision questions make classification unambiguous for 90%+ of scenarios
- [ ] Quick reference card section is present
- [ ] Reviewed by at least 2 engineers who will be on call
